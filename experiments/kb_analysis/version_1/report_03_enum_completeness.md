# Report 3 — per-ENUM completeness
**KB:** `version_1/feature-and-product-knowledge.local.csv` — all 198 fragments analyzed.
This is the exhaustive, per-fragment catalogue you asked for: *for each ENUM, is anything missing or under-explained enough to confuse the agent or make it non-deterministic for the same question?* Each fragment's `summary` + `notInScope` + `description` were read in full (Slovak). Where the exact missing fact can't be known from outside the bank, the **area** of the missing information is named instead — your colleagues can fill those in.
> Read alongside [Report 0](report_00_kb_data_quality.md) (the hard bugs, de-duplicated and source-verified) and [Report 1](report_01_enum_disjointness.md) (cross-fragment confusability). Issues here are graded by **determinism impact**: **HIGH** = the agent will likely answer the same question differently across runs, or cannot answer at all; **MED** = an incomplete but stable answer; **LOW** = minor.
## Summary
- **331** completeness issues across **179** fragments (**19** fragments had no issue).
- By determinism impact: **HIGH 61**, MED 193, LOW 77.
- By type: `missing_info` 138, `scope_mismatch` 53, `underspecified` 49, `ambiguous` 42, `internal_contradiction` 36, `outdated_or_unverifiable` 13.
- **51** fragments carry at least one HIGH-impact issue (detailed below).

The two dominant patterns (see Report 0 §3 for the first): **missing concrete numbers** (fees/limits/rates deferred to the external Sadzobník) and **summary-vs-body scope mismatch** (the `summary` promises content the `description` never delivers).

> **How to read the `missing_info` / `underspecified` items about fees, rates and limits.** By design these figures are **not** in the KB — they live in the official price list (Sadzobník), shown to the customer as a **clickable link they open**. So such an item is a real defect only when (a) the ENUM fragment cites the price list with **no link**, (b) it gives an **illustrative figure** that could be quoted as real, or (c) the **linked document's accuracy** is in doubt (a separate check). Where an auto-generated line says the agent *cannot read/browse* a PDF or that a rate is *unanswerable by design*, read it as *the figure isn't restated in the ENUM fragment — it's in the linked price list*. The genuinely missing knowledge here is the non-price-list items (procedures, eligibility, conditions).
